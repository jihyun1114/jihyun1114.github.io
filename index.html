About

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Minnesota working on Bayesian nonparametric methods for current-status survival data. My current research develops Dirichlet process mixture models that jointly model censored event times with high-dimensional omics covariates via latent factor structure, enabling flexible nonparametric clustering with full uncertainty quantification.

My methodological interests also span semiparametric survival models and group testing — including Bayesian estimation for group-tested current-status data with imperfect diagnostic tests — alongside MCMC algorithms and variable selection in high dimensions. Applications include infectious disease epidemiology and population-scale public health screening.

I hold a PhD in Statistics from the University of South Carolina (GPA 3.97) and an MS from Ewha Womans University, where I also double-majored in Psychology.

A Unified Bayesian Approach for Regression Analysis of Group-Tested Current Status Data from Group Testing Studies

Jihyun Kim, Lianming Wang, Christopher S. McMahan, Joshua M. Tebbs, Shuwei Li, Tao Hu

Manuscript in Preparation

A unified Bayesian framework for analyzing group-tested current status data from any pool testing design — including master pool, Dorfman, and array testing. Models disease onset via a semiparametric probit model with monotone splines, handles imperfect tests with unknown assay accuracies, and provides a computationally efficient Gibbs sampler. Validated on chlamydia screening data from the University of Iowa.

Bayesian Semiparametric Current-Status Omics Model with Dirichlet Process Mixture and Slice Sampling

Jihyun Kim et al.

In Preparation · University of Minnesota

A joint Bayesian nonparametric model for current-status survival outcomes with high-dimensional omics covariates. Places a Dirichlet process mixture prior over cluster-specific survival and latent factor distributions, with spike-and-slab variable selection on omics loadings. Develops and compares three scalable posterior computation strategies: truncated DP, dependent slice sampler (Walker 2007), and independent slice sampler (Kalli et al. 2011).
